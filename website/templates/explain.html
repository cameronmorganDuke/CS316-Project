Variables Explained

  • Capitalization rate (cap rate) is a metric used to estimate the potential rate of return on an income-generating real estate investment property. It's calculated by dividing the property's net operating income (NOI) by its asset value and expressed as a percentage.
  • Net operating income (NOI) is a metric that measures the profitability of an investment property by subtracting operating expenses from total income.
  • Monthly rent is the amount of money paid to a landlord on a monthly basis in exchange for the right to live in a rental property.
  • Rental income is typically considered unearned income by tax authorities like the Internal Revenue Service (IRS).
  • Expenses means the costs (including, but not limited to, payroll, taxes, assessments, insurance, utilities, landscaping and other similar charges) of operating and maintaining any Property
  • Zoning is the local law that regulates how property can be used
  • Square footage of a property is a measurement of its living space calculated in square feet.
  • Property value refers to the estimated worth of a real estate asset, determined by factors like its location, size, condition, and market demand
  • Number of beds in a home refers to the total count of bedrooms within a house
  • Acre is a unit of land measurement that is equal to 43,560 square feet, or 4,840 square yards per acre.
  • Extraterritorial jurisdiction (ETJ) is the legal authority of a state to exercise power outside of its territory
  • Number of units in housing refers to the total count of separate living quarters within a building or property
  • Vacancy allowance percentage refers to the estimated percentage of potential rental income a property owner might lose due to units being vacant
  • Is gas means whether a home is equipped with natural gas lines, allowing it to use gas appliances like a furnace for heating, a water heater for hot water, and a stove or oven for cooking
  • Legal costs for housing can include attorney fees, title insurance, and other closing costs
  • Utility costs for housing refer to the cost of running a home, such as electricity, gas, water, sewage, and garbage collection.
  • A management reserve is a portion of a project budget that's set aside for unexpected work or risks that are within the project's scope.
  • Maintenance reserve , also known as a repair fund, is a set amount of money that's saved to cover unexpected expenses for a property.
  • Property tax is an annual or semiannual charge levied by a local government and paid by the owners of real estate within its jurisdiction.
